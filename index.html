About Cayman Brac

Cayman Brac a Nature Lover’s paradise is 12 miles long and 1 mile wide and has the most dramatic topography of the three Cayman Islands. Its majestic Bluff rises west to east along the island’s length to 152 feet above Spot Bay. Many mysterious caves are carved throughout this awe-inspiring natural attraction. The land and seascape offer visitors an ideal environment for outdoor activities such as hiking, rock climbing, birding, caving, cycling, fishing and scuba diving.

Home to some of the best dive sites in the world. The Bluff provides a natural barrier between the North Side and the open sea, making calm, clear waters ideal for diving and snorkelling. Dramatic drop-offs, swim-throughs and caves make it a mecca for divers of all levels.

Fishing is also excellent both from the shore and by boat. The deep-sea angler can test his skills against a blue marlin, tuna, wahoo, dolphin and barracuda. Reef fishing provides ample opportunity to catch grouper, snapper and other delicious fish.

Key Attractions

The Bluff

The Bluff is a popular destination in Cayman Brac. The steep cliffs offer stunning views of the ocean and the nearby reefs. Visitors can also enjoy hiking, bird watching, and picnicking in the area. The Bluff stands 152 feet above sea level and is the highest point on the island. The views from the top are truly breathtaking.

MV Captain Keith Tibbetts

MV Captain Keith Tibbetts was a retired Cuban spy ship that was sunk as an artificial reef off the coast in 1996. The wreck is now a popular dive site and home to a variety of marine life. MV Captain Keith Tibbetts is a great example of how something that was once used for negative purposes can be transformed into something positive and beneficial for the environment.

Parrot Preserve

This stunning Island is home to one of the Cayman's most unique and beloved residents – the Cayman Brac parrot. This vibrant little bird is found nowhere else in the world, and is an key part of Cayman’s natural heritage. The Cayman Brac parrot is endangered, but due to the efforts of dedicated conservationists, its population is slowly beginning to rebound. The Cayman Brac Parrot Reserve is a key part of this effort, providing a safe haven for these birds to thrive. Visitors to the Parrot Reserve can learn about the work being done to save these special birds, and even get up close and personal with them on guided tours.

Cayman Brac Caves

Cayman Brac has a number of caves to explore, perfect for the explorer! The Cayman Islands are home to a variety of unique caves, many of which are waiting to be discovered. Cayman Brac is the perfect place to explore these caves, with a number of easy-to-find and accessible caves. Whether you’re looking for a challenge or just want to take in the beauty of Cayman’s caves, Cayman Brac is the perfect destination.

Just a short flight from Grand Cayman…

Just a short flight from Grand Cayman, and several minutes from Little Cayman, Cayman Brac is known for its breath-taking bluff. Small towns here include the West End, Watering Place, Cotton Tree Bay, Creek and Spot Bay. Limestone caves and sinkholes contrast waters containing inspiring, unique dives along seawalls and even a sunken ship.

Captain Keith’s Tibbets 330-foot Frigate wreck is the only Russian warship that divers can explore in the Western hemisphere. And if you catch a fish or two along the way, you can bring it back to your resort to have the chef cook it for you Cayman-style.
